What is tifo?

Tifo is a phenomenon where fans, known as "tifosi" in Italian, celebrate their team by creating organized displays in the stands, primarily within organized football. Originating in Italy and Southern Europe in the late 1960s and early 1970s, tifo culture involves supporters meeting to systematically show their support during away matches through banners and flags. Common elements in tifo include confetti, fabric mosaics, streamers, balloons, flares, smoke bombs, banners, and flags. Tifo work is typically carried out by the team's tifo group or ultras.

Who are Djurgårdens IF?

Djurgårdens IF Fotbollsförening, based in Stockholm, has a rich football history. The men's team has a total of twelve Swedish championships. Djurgården, also known as Djurgår'n or DIF, has had significant periods of success in the 1910s, 1950s, 1960s, and 2000s. The club, with over 25,000 members as of late 2023, plays its home matches at Tele2 Arena since 2013, following a historic tenure at Stockholms Stadion. The team is nicknamed Järnkaminerna or Blåränderna.

Who are Sofiläktaren Tifo?

"Sofialäktaren Tifo" is the current tifo group for Djurgårdens IF, formed with the goal of executing terrace choreographies, including confetti, mosaic displays, overhead flags, pyrotechnics, etc., during Djurgården's matches. The group was established in 2013, coinciding with the move from Stockholms Stadion to Tele2 Arena. "Fabriken Stockholm," the former tifo group, was initially formed in 2005 but was disbanded before the 2013 relocation and subsequently renamed "Sofialäktaren Tifo".
